Endgame Principles

Look ahead and navigate to a winning endgame, or grind down your opponents and squeeze victory from a drawn position.

Create Passed Pawns

Passed pawns have no enemy pawns ahead that can stop them. Create passed pawns and push them to threaten promotion and put pressure on your opponents postiion.

Put your Rooks on the Seventh/Second Rank (Row)

Pawns can't protect themselves from the side or behind. By placing rooks on the seventh rank (or second rank if you play black), you get behind the pawns and outflank your opponent.

Bishop for Split Pawns

When pawns are on two opposite sides of the board, a bishop is better because it is quick and can fight on both sides.

Knight for Bunched Pawns

When pawns are on one side of the board, a knight is better because it can fight on both colors.
