Opening Principles

These tips will help you avoid early blunders and get a strong position to start the game!

Control the Center

Play happens in the center of the board. Occupying the center with pawns early on is a good way to make sure that you dont get cramped later on and gives you room to support them with your pieces.

Don't Move the Same Piece Twice in the Opening

A good way to have an advantage is to have more pieces. If you move the same piece twice, your oponent may use their move to bring another piece into the center and outnumber you there.

Dont Bring your Queen out Early

The queen is your most powerfull piece. This means that when it gets attacked it almost always has to move. Bringing out the queen early lets your opponent bring out their own pieces while attacking your queen, making you waste time and fall behind in development.

Castle Quickly

Castling early on gets your king to safety and prevents it from becoming trapped in the center where it can be attacked. It also helps bring your rooks into the game which can otherwise get stuck in the corners. Dont put off castling!
